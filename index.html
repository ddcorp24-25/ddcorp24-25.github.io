--Vestige--
A smart box for analyzing sediments. Our project is part of the FIRST LEGO LEAGUE competition. This year, the challenge given to teams was to imagine a solution capable of helping archaeologists in their work.

The Origin of the Project
So we didn't start with archaeology by chance: it was the competition's theme that led us to take an interest in this field. To better understand the real needs in the field, we decided to talk directly with professionals.

This allowed us to talk with archaeologists such as Fawzi Doumaz and Daniel Istria. Through several exchanges and video calls with them, we discovered how much sediments are an essential source of information for understanding an archaeological site.

However, analyzing these sediments often requires time, experience, and sometimes sending samples to a laboratory. So we looked for an idea that could facilitate and speed up this important step in archaeologists' work.

Our Solution: An Automated Analysis Box
We came up with the idea of a box capable of automatically analyzing a sediment sample.

The principle is simple: the archaeologist places a sediment sample in the box, which then goes through several analysis steps, somewhat like a scientific examination chain.

Inside the box, a small conveyor belt moves the sediment through different sensors. At each step, a new sensor analyzes a characteristic of the sediment.

Thus, within a few minutes, several important pieces of information can be automatically retrieved. This solution would work like a mini portable laboratory directly in the field.

The Different Analysis Steps
When the sediment is placed in the box, it gradually moves along the conveyor belt and passes through several analysis stations.

  • Compactness Analysis – Measures soil resistance using a penetrometer to understand the layer structure.
  • Color Analysis – A colorimetry sensor detects the presence of organic matter or old deposits.
  • pH Analysis – Indicates whether the soil is acidic, neutral, or basic, which affects the preservation of archaeological objects.
  • Position Recording – Data can be linked to a GPS position to connect information to the site map.

Why This System Can Be Useful
Our analysis box would allow quickly obtaining several scientific pieces of information about a sediment, directly in the field.

  • Analyze a sediment in just a few minutes
  • Automatically obtain multiple data points
  • Facilitate the work of archaeologists
  • Better understand the different soil layers
  • Reduce waiting time for certain analyses

In summary, our goal is to create a tool that would make sediment analysis faster, simpler, and more efficient.
